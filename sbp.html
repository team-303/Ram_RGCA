Seabass Hatchery Project

The Seabass Hatchery Project was initiated during 1999 at Thoduvai village of Sirkali-Taluk, Nagapattinam-District, Tamil Nadu.

Major objective

To develop technologies for large scale Seabass seed production

To develop Larval & Nursery rearing technologies

To disseminate technologies to Entrepreneurs/Govt. organizations

Salient features of the facility

#1

The seabass brooders are maintaining in Recirculation Aquaculture System (RAS) and selected male and females are maintaining in controlled environment tanks which is connected to the heater and chiller units.

#2

Year-round production of Algae, Rotifer and larval rearing is standardized.

#3

In larval rearing section improved survival from 30 % to 80% which was done by improving live feed operations and protocol for weaning diet.

#4

The weaned fry/fingerlings are supplying to the farmers/entrepreneurs and conducting several hands-on training programmes for the importance of larval rearing

#5

Perfected in the RAS system in Broodstock Housing Facility.

#6

Rotifer production technology improved and standardized to produce rotifer of about 150-200 nos/ml in all the seasons in out-door section.

#7

Regular hands on training programmes are offering on Seabass, Mud crab nursery and farming practices which is maintaining by RGCA head office, extension department.

#8

RGCA offering national level consultancy services for Seabass Seed production technology to the private entrepreneurs and govt. fisheries departments.

Achievements

Nearly, 17 million seeds/fingerlings were supplied to the aquafarmers, farmers, Research Institutes & other beneficiaries since inception.
