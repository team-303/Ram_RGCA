Mangrove Mud crab Hatchery Project

The Mangrove Mud Crab Hatchery Project was initiated during 2004 at Thoduvai village of Sirkali-Taluk, Nagapattinam-District, Tamil Nadu.

Objective

To develop technologies for large scale seed production of mangrove mud crab (Scylla serrata).

To develop Larval & Nursery rearing technologies for Indian conditions

To disseminate technologies to Entrepreneurs/Govt. organizations

Salient features of the facility

#1

The Mud Crab Hatchery Project has successfully developed the technology for mass hatchery production of Mud Crab (Scylla serrata) seed.

#2

The project has achieved record survival rates as high as 17% in few batches and consistently achieving 4.0-4.3% in hatchery operations against the world average of 3.5%.

#3

Year-round production of Algae and Rotifer has been standardized. Algae strains maintaining in this facility are Nannochloropsis, Isochrysis, Tetraselmis and Chlorella. Rotifer (Brachionus sp.) and Artemia (A. franciscana) cyst & biomass also maintaining in this facility.

#4

Enrichment of rotifer/Artemia is perfected by using 5 different algal strains to increase the health of the larvae and survival.

#5

Rotifer production technology improved and standardized to produce rotifer of about 150-200 nos/ml in all the seasons in out-door section.

#6

Regular hands on training programmes are offering on Seabass, Mud crab nursery and farming practices which is maintaining by RGCA head office, extension department.

#7

RGCA offering national level consultancy services for Seabass Seed production technology to the private entrepreneurs and govt. fisheries departments.
