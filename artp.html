Artemia Project

RGCA has established an integrated Artemia production unit for the first time in India and started cyst production trials by 2007 at Tharuvaikulam, Tuticorin, Tamil Nadu. In 2014, RGCA developed Artemia demonstration farm at Uppoor, Ramanathapuram, Tamil Nadu.

Objectives /Mandates

To develop and standardize the technology for good quality Artemia cyst and biomass production

To disseminate the Artemia cyst production and farming technology through technology transfer programmes.

Salient features of the facility

#1

Artemia, commonly known as “Brine Shrimp” is a unique cosmopolitan anostracan (one of the four orders of crustaceans in the class Branchiopoda) living in hypersaline and saline lakes, ponds, lagoons and man-made salterns with salinity levels ranging from 10 to 340 g/l.

#2

Artemia is a very good live feed that has revolutionized aquaculture industry globally and being used for more than 85% of aquaculture species, mainly in shrimp hatcheries.

#3

Annual harvests from Great Salt Lake, Utah were in the order of 2000 tonnes of dry cyst making up more than 90 percent of the world’s cyst market.

#4

The abundance of Indian native species A. parthenogenetica (asexual species) is very rare since 2002 and now vastly replaced with A. franciscana (bisexual species) in natural habitats which may be escaped from local shrimp hatcheries who imported the cysts sourced from Great Salt Lake, Utah, USA.

#5

The cyst produced from RGCA’s units has good hatching rate of 90-95%.

#6

RGCA is providing hands-on training in Artemia cyst and biomass production to empower the Socio-economic status of coastal community women group and more than 50 participants benefitted by the training till now.

A comparative study of fatty acid profile of RGCA Pearl brand artemia cyst with the imported commercial brand revealed that pearl brand cysts of RGCA contained higher levels of fatty acids namely C12:0 Lauric Acid (0.02g/100g); C14:0 Myristic Acid(0.03g/100g); C16:0 Palmitic Acid (0.20g/100g); C16:1 Palmitoleic Acid (0.09g/100g); C17:1 Cis-10-Heptadecanoic Acid (0.02g/100g); C18:1 Oleic Acid (0.13g/100g); C18:2 Lenoleic acid (0.01g/100g); Saturated Fatty Acids (0.31g/100g); Mono Unsaturated Fatty Acids (0.23g/100g) compared to imported commercial brand of artemia cysts except C18:0 Stearic Acid 0.06g/100g). Therefore, it is recommended to use it in aqua-larviculture as the presence of highly unsaturated fatty acids is a key factor that determines the nutritional value and marketing of Artemia for shrimp, marine larvae, sturgeon and ornamental fish industries.

Achievements

Since inception, nearly 1300 Tins of dry cyst (each tin contains 425 g dry cysts) and 7.12 tons of Artemia biomass were produced.

From Uppoor Demofarm, 85 Tins of dry cyst and 56 Kg of Artemia biomass were produced.
