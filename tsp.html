Domestication of Tiger Shrimp Project

Domestication of Tiger Shrimp Project of RGCA is located at Kodiyaghat, Port Blair, Andaman &Nicobar Islands. The project was initiated during 2005 and the constructions of NBC facilities were completed during the year 2013. The Domestication of Tiger Shrimp Project was inaugurated on 28/02/2014 and full-fledged operation of DTSP was started thereafter.

Objectives /Mandates

To produce Specific Pathogen Free (SPF) tiger shrimp (Penaeus monodon) broodstocks which are free from all known tiger shrimp diseases which are known/likely to cause mortality/crop loss to shrimp farmers.

To domesticate the SPF tiger shrimp and during the process of domestication efforts shall be made to improve desirable characteristics such as growth rate, fecundity, fertility etc

To enable the shrimp export industry to comply with norms related to Traceability issues which is a necessity for getting access into many new and also to retain the existing shrimp markets.

Salient features of the facility

#1

RGCA has created world class infrastructure facilities that are technologically advanced and with best possible bio-security.

#2

This is the first project of its kind in India and probably the most sophisticated and technologically advanced projects among five Tiger Shrimp domestication programmes across the world.r and larval rearing is standardized.

#3

DTSP has Pre-Primary and Primary Quarantine Unit at Amkunj, Middle Andaman to collect broodstock for initial quarantine for the production of founder families.
