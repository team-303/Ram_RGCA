Tilapia Project

Tilapia (GIFT) Project of RGCA is located at Manikonda village in Krishna-District of Andhra Pradesh. The project initiated during 2008.

Objectives

To standardize Hatchery and Grow out farming technology of Genetically Improved Farmed Tilapia (GIFT strain; Oreochromis niloticus).

To produce All Male seeds of GIFT strain and to supply to aquafarmers and entrepreneurs.

Conducting selective breeding programme of GIFT to produce quality seeds and broodstocks.

To support Satellite Breeding Centre for GIFT in India by providing improved GIFT stocks.

Providing consultancy services on GIFT propagation in India.

Salient features of the facility

#1

Tilapia commonly known as ‘Aquatic Chicken’ is one of the most popular seafood commodities across the world with a growing global market of 11 to 12% per annum and FAO described Tilapia as one of the most important food fish of the 21st century.

#2

RGCA is successfully conducting pedigreed breeding programme of GIFT in Indian condition and produced G8 generation.

#3

RGCA providing technical assistance for establishment of GIFT Satellite Breeding Centers and bio-secured tilapia farms

#4

RGCA is conducting regular hands-on training programmes in GIFT breeding, seed production & grow-out farming.

Graph showing GIFT seed supplied and numbers of beneficiaries from RGCA

Highlights of Genetically Improved Farmed Tilapia (GIFT) seeds of RGCA

Fully pedigreed selectively bred stocks

Tested for TiLV regularly and produce are free from TiLV

High growth rate, survival rate and better fillet yield

Average genetic gain of 8.8% in each generation

FCR is 1 : 0.85% in 5th generation

Suitable for both low density and intensive culture systems

Good quality flesh

Achievements

Since inception 13.2 million all male seeds and 6500 nos of Broodstock fry were supplied to research Institutes & other beneficiaries.
