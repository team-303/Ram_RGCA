Grouper Project

Grouper Project of RGCA was initiated during 2006 at Kodiyaghat, Port Blair, Andaman & Nicobar Islands.

Objectives /Mandates

To develop hatchery technology for high valued groupers

To develop technology for grow–out culture of groupers in floating net cages to marketable size through better management practices.

To transfer technology so developed by RGCA to the local fishermen, entrepreneurs, self-help groups in association with Department of Fisheries, A & N Administration.

Salient features of the facility

#1

Grouper or cods are commercially very important marine food fishes and are very expensive species.

#2

The export market for grouper is rapidly increasing especially in Hong Kong, Singapore and Japan. Live harvested fish are fetching much higher price (US$ 30/kg) than the frozen (US$ 4/KG) in the market.

#3

RGCA is providing Consultancy services for the establishment of Grouper Hatchery and providing technical guidance for the hatchery and grow-out practices of Tiger Grouper.

#4

The project is initiated to supply of Tiger Grouper seeds for aquaculture entrepreneurs.

Achievements

Successfully developed technology for conditioning the grouper broodstock in open sea net cage system which is the first of its kind in India.

Standardized technology for hatchery seed production of tiger grouper.

Demonstration of cage farming of tiger grouper from the hatchery produced fingerlings for the first time in India.

Hatchery production of Epinephelus coioides (Orange spotted grouper) fingerlings.

Achieved 62% of survival in farming of tiger grouper in floating net cages and also attained 5.7% of survival for hatchery production of tiger grouper fries.

Conducted awareness programme on cage culture of tiger grouper in floating net cages.
